\begin{abstract}
Federated Learning (FL) for IoT intrusion detection (IDS) is bottlenecked both by the bandwidth budget of edge devices and by the accuracy degradation that occurs when client traffic distributions are heterogeneous. Recent work has integrated Kolmogorov--Arnold Networks (KANs) into FL outside the IDS setting; we present \textbf{FedKAN-IDS}, the first systematic, parameter-matched empirical study of Federated KANs on three standardised NetFlow-v2 intrusion detection datasets (NF-BoT-IoT-v2, NF-ToN-IoT-v2, NF-CSE-CIC-IDS2018-v2), each evaluated with $n = 10$ seeds under three heterogeneity regimes and both binary and multi-class detection. Our contribution is empirical rather than methodological, and we are careful to scope it. \textbf{(i)} On \emph{binary} detection under extreme heterogeneity (Dir $\alpha = 0.1$) FedKAN-8 ($\sim 3.3$k parameters) achieves a $+5$ to $+6$ percentage-point mean advantage over a parameter-matched FedAvg-MLP on the two most class-skewed datasets, with the BoT-IoT-v2 worst-seed gap reaching $+24$ pp and a $2.6\times$ variance reduction; doubling MLP capacity does not close the gap, supporting an architectural rather than capacity-driven reading. \textbf{(ii)} On \emph{multi-class} detection, FedKAN-8 attains a seed-paired $+1.73$~pp advantage on the most class-diverse dataset (CSE-CIC-IDS2018-v2) with $p = 0.012$ and bootstrap $95\%$ CI $[+0.73, +2.74]$ excluding zero --- the most rigorous single positive result in our study, and consistent with the prediction that each additional class amplifies the cumulative regularising effect of the KAN spline-bias term. \textbf{(iii)} We identify a reproducible failure mode: seed~17's Dirichlet partition drives FedKAN-8 below the parameter-matched MLP on two of three datasets, mitigated by either larger hidden width (F-KAN-16 escapes the trap) or a richer spline grid ($G\!=\!10$ recovers $\sim 5$ percentage points). All observations are anchored in a convergence analysis showing the KAN B-spline approximation introduces an additive $\mathcal{O}(G^{-(k+1)})$ bias term independent of the heterogeneity factor $\Gamma_\alpha$. Code, configurations, and $289$ reproducible runs are publicly available.
\end{abstract}
