\textbf{Contributions.} This paper makes the following contributions.
\begin{itemize}
\item We present the first systematic, parameter-matched empirical comparison of Federated Kolmogorov--Arnold Networks (FedKAN) against FedAvg-MLP on the three standardised NetFlow-v2 IDS datasets, under both binary and multi-class detection and three data-heterogeneity regimes (IID, Dirichlet $\alpha\in\{1.0, 0.1\}$).
\item We identify a robustness/tail-risk advantage of FedKAN that is architectural rather than capacity-driven: a parameter-matched MLP suffers worst-seed accuracy drops of $\sim 24$ percentage points relative to FedKAN-8 under extreme non-IID, and doubling MLP capacity does not close this gap.
\item We anchor the empirical finding in a convergence analysis (Theorem \ref{thm:conv}) that introduces a spline-bias term $\mathcal{O}(G^{-(k+1)})$ which is independent of the data-heterogeneity factor and acts as an effective regulariser.
\item All code, dataset preparation scripts, and 146 reproducible runs are released at \url{https://github.com/haodpsut/FedKAN-IDS-v2}.
\end{itemize}
