We have presented FedKAN-IDS, the first systematic parameter-matched empirical comparison of Federated Kolmogorov--Arnold Networks against FedAvg-MLP on NetFlow-based IoT intrusion detection. Across 146 reproducible runs on NF-BoT-IoT-v2 (and forthcoming NF-ToN-IoT-v2), we identified an architectural robustness advantage of FedKAN under extreme client heterogeneity that is not replicated by simply scaling up MLP capacity, anchored in a convergence theorem that introduces a spline-approximation bias term independent of the data-heterogeneity factor. Our analysis explicitly walks back the ``$\sim 50\%$ communication reduction'' claim of prior FedKAN-IDS work, showing that under parameter parity FedKAN incurs roughly $14\%$ more bytes per round than its MLP counterpart, and reframes the case for FedKAN around tail-risk reduction (worst-seed accuracy), which is the more deployment-relevant metric for IDS. Future work includes coupling FedKAN with grid-sparsification compression in the spirit of CG-FKAN \cite{yu2025cgfkan}, and physical edge deployment on Raspberry Pi-class gateways.
